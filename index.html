Hello! I'm Vivian Garcia.

I am the research coordinator for Dr. Andrew Lynn's Attention in NeuroDevelopment (AND) Lab at the University of Louisville. I am involved in studies investigating how children pay attention to the world and how this shapes their math and reading skills. This means that I get to code a lot of cool experiments, like one that tracks gaze to make sure that children are always looking at the center of the screen! Right now, I am also leading a project using geocoded variables and neuroimaging data from the Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development Study to examine the effects of early environmental heat exposure on brain structure development in collaboration with Dr. Charlie Zhang in the Department of Geographic & Environmental Sciences.

I graduated from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 2024 with a B.S. in Brain and Cognitive Sciences and a minor in Anthropology. While at MIT, I worked with Dr. Ashley Thomas in Dr. Rebecca Saxe's Social & Developmental Cognitive Neuroscience Lab to study children's expectations about sharing and altruism in parent-child relationships. I also spent some time in Dr. Laura Schulz's Early Childhood Cognition Lab, designing an experiment to learn more about how children and infants integrate auditory and visual cues to infer the properties of objects. I am very interested in how children reason about social and physical events, and what cues they use to guide their inferences. For example, did you know that infants infer that people who share saliva (e.g., by licking the same spoon) have a close relationship (Thomas et al., 2022)? I hope to further explore children's multimodal sensory integration, social and physical reasoning, and the role of early experiences in shaping the development of these domains in grad school!
